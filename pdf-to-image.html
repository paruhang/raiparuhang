Frequently Asked Questions

Are my PDF files uploaded to a server?
No. Everything runs inside your browser using PDF.js, a Mozilla open-source library. Your PDF never leaves your device. No uploads, no servers, no accounts needed.
What's the difference between JPG, PNG, and WebP?
JPG uses lossy compression — great for photos and smaller file sizes. PNG is lossless — perfect for sharp text, diagrams, or transparency. WebP is a modern format that achieves excellent quality at the smallest file size, supported by all modern browsers.
What DPI should I use?
72–96 DPI for screen/web use. 150 DPI is a good general-purpose choice. 200–300 DPI is ideal for printing or when you need sharp, crisp images for editing. Higher DPI produces larger files and takes longer to process.
Can I extract only specific pages?
Yes. Use the Page Range field to specify exactly which pages you want — for example "1-3, 5, 8-10". You can also use the quick presets: All, First, Last, Odd, or Even pages.
Is there a page or file size limit?
No server-side limits. The only constraint is your device's RAM and browser memory. Very large PDFs at 300 DPI can consume significant memory. For heavy files, use 150 DPI or extract pages in smaller batches using the page range feature.
