How To

How to Use the Percentage Calculator

Each tab solves a different percentage problem. Here's how each one works and when to use it.

01

Find percentage of a number

Enter the percentage and the total amount. The calculator multiplies the number by the percentage rate to give you the exact value. Use this to find a discount amount, tax value, or any portion of a number.

Result = (X ÷ 100) × Y
02

Work out percentage between two numbers

Enter the partial value (X) and the total (Y). Divides X by Y and multiplies by 100. Perfect for test score percentages, market share, or how much of a goal you've hit.

Result = (X ÷ Y) × 100
03

Calculate percentage increase or decrease

Enter the original value and the new value. A positive result means an increase; negative means a decrease. Useful for price changes, salary growth, or comparing any two stats over time.

((New − Old) ÷ |Old|) × 100
04

Calculate percentage of a percentage

Enter two percentage values. The tool multiplies them and divides by 100. Useful for compound discounts — for example, 20% of a 50% discount equals 10%.

(X × Y) ÷ 100
05

Add or remove a percentage from a number

Enter the base number and the percentage, then choose add or remove. Use "Add %" for price after tax or tip. Use "Remove %" to find the pre-discount price or reverse a percentage applied.

Add: N × (1 + P/100) · Remove: N × (1 − P/100)
06

Copy and use your result

Every result includes a Copy button. Click it to copy the value to your clipboard instantly. The formula used is shown below the result so you always know exactly how it was calculated.

Works entirely in your browser
