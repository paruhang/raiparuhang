Frequently Asked Questions

How do I find the aspect ratio of an image or video?
Enter your image's width and height — leave the ratio fields blank. The calculator uses the GCD (greatest common divisor) to simplify the ratio automatically. For example, 1920 × 1080 simplifies to 16:9, and 800 × 600 simplifies to 4:3.
How do I calculate height from width and a ratio?
Enter your width value, then set the ratio (either by typing in the ratio fields or clicking a preset). The height is calculated as width × ratio_H ÷ ratio_W. Calculated fields are highlighted in amber so you know which value was derived.
What aspect ratio should I use for YouTube videos?
Use 16:9 for standard YouTube videos — recommended at 1920×1080 (Full HD) or 3840×2160 (4K). For YouTube Shorts, Reels, and TikTok, use 9:16 vertical at 1080×1920.
What is the best aspect ratio for social media?
It depends on the platform: Instagram feed posts work best at 1:1 (square) or 4:5 (portrait). Stories and Reels use 9:16. Twitter/X headers use roughly 3:1. Facebook cover photos are approximately 820:312.
What is the difference between 16:9 and 16:10?
16:9 (1.78:1) is the universal standard for TV, online video, and most monitors. 16:10 (1.6:1) is slightly taller — common in older MacBooks and professional monitors — giving a bit more vertical space for documents and code. Most modern content targets 16:9.
